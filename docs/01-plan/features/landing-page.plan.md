# PDCA Plan: Landing Page for Kim Hong-nyeon

## 1. Overview
Create a personal landing page for elementary school teacher Kim Hong-nyeon (김홍년).
The page will serve as a portfolio and introduction to his work in "Vibe Coding" and "Generative AI" education.
The primary goal is to showcase his expertise, mission, and lecture offerings to other teachers and educators.

## 2. Goals
- **Personal Branding:** Establish Kim Hong-nyeon as an expert in AI and Vibe Coding education.
- **Information Delivery:** Clearly communicate his mission (workload reduction, educational enhancement).
- **Engagement:** Encourage teachers to learn more about his lectures and programs.

## 3. Requirements

### Content
- **Hero Section:**
    - Name: 김홍년 (Kim Hong-nyeon)
    - Role: 초등학교 교사 (Elementary School Teacher)
    - Tagline/Headline emphasizing Vibe Coding & Generative AI.
- **About Me:**
    - Brief bio.
    - Passion for reducing teacher workload and enhancing student education through technology.
- **Expertise / Areas of Focus:**
    - **Vibe Coding:** Explanation/Showcase.
    - **Generative AI:** How it's used in education.
    - **Program Development:** Mention of tools developed for teachers/students.
- **Lectures & Workshops:**
    - Information about lectures for teachers on AI utilization and Vibe Coding.
- **Contact / Footer:**
    - Links to social media, email, or a contact form.

### Design & UX
- **Theme:** "Vibe Coding" & "Generative AI".
- **Style:** Modern, clean, tech-forward but accessible (friendly to teachers).
- **Visuals:** Use abstract code elements, AI-generated imagery style, or vibrant colors associated with "Vibe".
- **Responsiveness:** Must work well on mobile and desktop.

## 4. Constraints
- **Platform:** Static website (likely Next.js via Starter skill).
- **Language:** Korean (Primary).

## 5. Success Criteria
- The landing page effectively communicates Kim Hong-nyeon's identity and mission.
- The design reflects the "Vibe Coding" and "AI" theme.
- All sections (Hero, About, Expertise, Lectures, Contact) are present and functional.
